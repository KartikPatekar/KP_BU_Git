\subsection*{Part a}

Suppose bank overserves the entrepreneur type $p \in \{p_l, p_h\}$ and sets corresponding rate $r \in \{r_l, r_h\}$. Bank's expected payoff conditional on making the loan is 

\begin{align*}
    EU^B(\text{Offer loan}) = p (1+r) L
\end{align*}

By zero expected profit condition, $EU^B(\text{Offer loan}) = EU^B(\text{Not offer loan})$, and hence the interest rates offered are 
\begin{align*}
    r_h = \frac{1}{p_h} - 1~~~, ~~~ r_l = \frac{1}{p_l} - 1
\end{align*}

Expected payoff for entrepreneur if they take the loan is 

\begin{align*}
    EU^E(\text{Take loan})  = p(S - (1+r)L) + (1-p)D
\end{align*}

Entrepreneur takes the loan if $EU^E(\text{Take loan}) \ge EU^E(\text{Not take loan}) $, which gives 
\begin{align*}
    p(S - L/p) + (1-p)D &\ge 0 \\
    \implies p &\ge \frac{L-D}{S-D}
\end{align*}

Since this condition is satisfied for both types of entrepreneurs, all the entrepreneurs will take the loan.

\subsection*{Part b}

Bank can only set one interest rate $r$. 

An entrepreneur with success probability $p$ will accept the loan if 
\begin{align*}
    EU^E(\text{Take loan}) &\ge EU^E(\text{Not take loan}) \\
    \implies p(S - (1+r)L) + (1-p)D &\ge 0\\
    \implies \frac{1}{1+r} \ge \frac{p L}{p S + (1-p)D}
\end{align*}

Since $S-D > L > 0$, the RHS is incresaing in $p$. Hence, if good types take the loan, then bad types also take the loan, but not vice versa.

\textbf{Case 1: Both types of entrepreneurs take loan in equilibrium}\\

Bank's expected payoff conditional on making the loan is

\begin{align*}
    EU^B(\text{Offer loan}) &= [\lambda p_h  + (1- \lambda)p_l ] (1+r) L\\
    EU^B(\text{Offer loan}) &= EU^B(\text{Not offer loan}) \implies 1+r = \frac{1}{\lambda p_h  + (1- \lambda)p_l } 
\end{align*}

An entrepreneur with success probability $p$ will accept the loan if 
\begin{align*}
    \lambda p_h  + (1- \lambda)p_l \ge \frac{p L}{p S + (1-p)D}
\end{align*}

According to given condition, good entrepreneur does not take the loan. Therefore, a pooling equilibrium is not possible.

\textbf{Case 2: Only bad type entrepreneurs take loan in equilibrium}\\

As solved in part a, the bank sets the interest rate $r = \frac{1}{p_l}-1$. This is greater than interest rate in Case 1 above, and therefore only bad types take the loan while the good types do not take the loan.\\

Hence, there is a unique equilibrium which shows moral hazard problem. Due to incomplete information, bank cannot proftably lend to good entrepreneurs.
